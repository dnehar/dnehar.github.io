Research Focus

Mapping immunity at single-cell resolution

Our lab integrates single-cell transcriptomics, epigenomics, and systems-level computational analysis to understand how immune cell identities, states, and networks change across human development, aging, and disease.

🍼
Infant immune development

Infancy is the most immunologically dynamic period of life. We study unique infant immune features — including SOX4⁺ naïve T cells, constitutive ISG expression, and pDC predominance — to understand early-life immune programming.

🧬
SOX4⁺ T cell biology

We investigate the abundance, function, and epigenetic landscape of SOX4⁺ naïve T cells — a population enriched in infants that carries stemness signatures, thymic emigrant features, and a Th2 differentiation bias.

Computational tools & atlases

We develop open-access immune cell atlases and bioinformatics tools, including interactive Shiny apps and pipelines for single-cell data integration, cell type annotation, and CMV inference.

Our Team

The people behind the science

We are a diverse, collaborative team of immunologists and computational biologists at Emory University. We are currently building the lab and recruiting at all levels.

Djamel Nehar-Belaid, PhD
Principal Investigator
Dr. Nehar-Belaid is an expert in systems immunology and single-cell genomics. His work spans immune aging, early-life immunity, autoimmune disease, and vaccine biology. He trained in Dr. Jacques Banchereau's labs at The Jackson Laboratory before establishing his independent lab in the Department of Pathology and Laboratory Medicine at Emory University School of Medicine in September 2026.
